April 5, 2024

Photos of the week!

Leon is now seven weeks old and about as heavy as a black swan, so let's move on to some post hoc analysis of our favorite little world-altering event.

Leon's favorite new activity is unclenching his fists. It's nice to have a less intimidating baby. We even felt confident enough to have some friends over for dinner. But open hands means he spends more time tiny-fingered tickling the neck and ribcage of the person holding him. Life is full of tradeoffs.

We had a few nights this week where Leon couldn't sleep in his bassinet for more than thirty minutes straight. Then a sleep consultant suggested that we keep him upright for a while after feeding for the sake of gravity-aided digestion. Then he went back to sleeping normally. The logic of infancy is opaque and remorseless but, typically, sound.

Photos of the week!

Leon is now six weeks old and a bit heavier than a Nubian bustard.

March 22, 2024

Photos of the week!

As of this morning, Leon is five weeks old and about as heavy as a spot-billed pelican.

A neutral observer might struggle to identify significant Leon developments since last week's post. Luckily, we are not neutral observers, so we can honestly claim that Leon's smiles are becoming slightly less random, his vocalizations are acquiring the tenor of conversation, and he is this close to rolling over. Plus, one incontrovertible fact: he is a natural dancer (five-second video).

There was a night this week where Leon put together five consecutive hours of uninterrupted sleep. We woke up and discussed it like it was a gleaming alien spacecraft that had alighted overnight in our backyard and now lay silently under a thin sheet of dew. We whispered to each other in awe, curiosity, and a little fear, then agreed it was just a lucky break, then silently and irrationally constructed private fantasies of a baby that starts sleeping through the night after one month. It hasn't happened again, but we still dream.

Photos of the week!

Leon is now three weeks old! There's no pediatrician visit for this milestone. March 1, 2024

Photos of the week!

Today was Leon's two-week pediatrician appointment. He continues to be, medically speaking, a big ol' baby. The half-mile round trip was our first walk as a family (baby wraps are cool; I'm wearing Leon right now).

Leon is an enthusiastic participant in all of the human bodily functions. He sometimes reminds us of somebody who, having been placed in the cockpit of an airplane for the first time, decides to press every single button at once.

Matthew's parents visited this week. Having them here has been fun. Finding place for six adults and one baby in a 600 square foot apartment has been fun, too. Last night the grandparents kicked us out, so the two of us had a surprisingly relaxed dinner at the restaurant across the street.

We're slowly becoming less and less anxious as Leon patiently presents us with evidence that he's not as fragile as we think. We're also averaging 6-7 hours of sleep per 24 hours, though frankly, I hesitate to even write this fact down, since given what people had told us about newborns, that number seems like a random and unearned gift.

February 25, 2024

Photo of the week!

Leon is now over a week old! He has regained most of his birth weight and is feeding well. Marcella’s parents are nearby for a few weeks, and their help — especially the evening period when they take Leon into the living room and we put on earplugs and eye masks and make a deposit in the sleep bank — is appreciated.

So far, life feels kind of like it’s shrunk to a space about the size of our apartment. Sometimes that space just seems to be feeding and diapering and figuring out who is less dangerously close to falling asleep. But sometimes we’re staring at Leon and don’t know why we’d bother doing anything else. Overall, pretty good!
